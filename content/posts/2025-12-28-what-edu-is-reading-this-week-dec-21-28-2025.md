Here is the roundup of links for this week:

## Cloud & Infrastructure

* [**Debusine Repositories Launch**](https://linuxiac.com/debian-gets-its-own-ppa-like-system-as-debusine-repositories-launch/) / [**Main Page**](https://debusine.debian.net/): Debian introduces Debusine, a new PPA-like system allowing developers to publish add-on packages with integrated builds and testing.
* [**PCI Passthrough in Harvester**](https://www.suse.com/c/pci-passthrough-demystified-setting-up-amd-gpus-in-harvester/) - A guide on setting up AMD GPUs in Harvester with PCI passthrough.
* [**Password Agents**](https://systemd.io/PASSWORD_AGENTS/) - Documentation from systemd.io regarding the implementation and use of Password Agents.

## DevOps & Tools

* [**Snitch**](https://github.com/karol-broda/snitch) - A tool to inspect network connections in a cleaner, more visual way than standard utilities.
* [**RustScan**](https://github.com/bee-san/RustScan) - The "Modern Port Scanner" — a fast and efficient port scanner written in Rust.
* [**ezff**](http://npmjs.com/package/ezff) - A "plain English" wrapper for ffmpeg, designed to make complex media conversions easier to remember.
* [**textarea**](https://github.com/antonmedv/textarea) - A minimalist text editor that lives in URL.
* [**Antigravity Workspace Template**](https://github.com/study8677/antigravity-workspace-template) - A starter kit for the Google Antigravity IDE, optimized for Gemini 3 Agentic Workflows and "Deep Think" mode.

## SDR & Radio

* [**LTE-Cell-Scanner**](https://github.com/JiaoXianjun/LTE-Cell-Scanner) - An OpenCL-accelerated TDD/FDD LTE cell scanner compatible with RTL-SDR, HackRF, and BladeRF.
* [**fm_tune**](https://github.com/viraptor/fm_tune) - A utility to calculate rough PPM (Parts Per Million) error estimates for SDR devices using FM stations.
* [**kalibrate-rtl**](https://github.com/steve-m/kalibrate-rtl) - A fork of kalibrate designed specifically for use with RTL-SDR devices to calculate local oscillator frequency offsets.
